-Season Four: Part 02-

Episodes: 14-26

Episode 14: Silent Dust

A formula for a type of fertalizer that kills called 'Silent Dust' has been stolen. A small rural town hides a small plot for world domination. Steed brings out his inner horse girl and Peel investigates disappearences. This is my favorite episode of Season 4.

Episode 15: Room Without a View

A bevvy of government physicists are disappearing for months at a time, only to reappear completely brainwashed. The link? They all checked into the Chessman Hotel, Room 621, before their disappearences. It's up to Steed and Peel to go undercover and find out what nefarious business is happening at the prestigious Chessman hotel.

Episode 16: Small Game for Big Hunters

A gardner was found dead in full safari kit with a poisoned arrow in his back, in the middle of the English countryside. Soon, the duo investigate a cursed legend, a retired gentleman's officer club complete with massive greenhouse jungle, and a sinister plot drenched in colonial bullshit. This episode contains offesive stereotypes of a made up indigenous group.

Episode 17: The Girl From AUNTIE

Mrs. Peel has been kidnapped after a costume party and replaced by an actress, Ms. Georgie Pice Jones. After discovering the fraud, Steed and the actress join forces to find her. Knitting needle killers, suspicious art auctioneers, and vicious grannies all stand in the way of finding Mrs. Peel.

Episode 18: The Thirteenth Hole

A series of deaths leads back to a prestigious gentleman's golf club. Steed and Peel join the club to uncover it's secrets. Look out for stray golf balls flying a speeds high enough to kill!

Episode 19: Quick Quick Slow Death

A dead body in dancing shoes and full tuxedo is found, leading the duo to investigate a dance school and it's suspicious students.

Episode 20: The Danger Makers

Military Officers who miss the charge of war become adrenaline junkies in a deadly club called the 'Danger Makers.' But who's pulling the strings? Steed and Peel must go undercover to infiltrate the club and find out who's running the illegal operation.

Episode 21: A Touch of Brimstone

Someone is murding important diplomats, stirring up conflict between nations. It all ties back to a club that prides itself on being a 'den of sin.' The Hellfire Club and it's antics ended up getting the episode banned in Brittain for most of the 20th century due to their strict censorship laws.

Episode 22: What the Butler Saw

Someone is leaking government secrets, leaving these four military generals under suspician. What do they all have in common? They all hired butlers from the same school of butling of course!

Episode 23: The House that Jack Built

Mrs. Peel inherits an old house in the country from her uncle Jack. Accept she doesn't have an uncle Jack and when she goes inside she soon discovers that it's a maze of death designed to never be escaped from alive. Who built this house? Will Mrs. Peel outsmart the house and escape in time?

Episode 24: A Sense of History

A murderous group of college boys are targeting academic writers they disagree with. Steed and Peel go undercover as students at the boy's beautiful university to investigate the group and to find out who is directing them to kill. It's a fun week at the University as well, with a Robin Hood party hosting the climactic ending fight.

Episode 25: How to Succeed... at Murder

Secretaries are taking their bosses jobs after their untimely demises. A group of murderous secretaries taking their careers into their own hands and moving up in the world (by putting their employers down)? Sign me up for their club ASAP!

Episode 26: Honey for the Prince

A plot of an Arabian Prince visiting the country, a club called the QQF (Quite, Quite Fantastic) based around making your dreams a reality, and a large order of honey all tie this mystery together. This episode contains outdated stereotypes of fairytale arabian culture.
