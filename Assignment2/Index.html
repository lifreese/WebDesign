--- My History with Games: An Introduction---

When I was a kid, computers were at a point of becoming mainstays both in school and at home. The Apple Macintosh was king and every kid loved walking down to computer lab during the school week. The internet was slow and off limits to us kids. So what did we have when boredom came a knockin? COMPUTER GAMES! On this page, I've created a mini repository of old games that I loved to play as a child. Now to specify, I won't be speaking on browser games, RIP Adobe Flash, but rather games that came on these things called CD ROMS. Also, due to time constraints, I will only be discussing four of the games that I would consider core to my early gaming experience. Those games are, 'Zoo Tycoon,' 'Secrets of DaVinci; The Forbidden Manuscript,' 'Marble Blast! Gold,' and 'Bugdom.' Did you play any of these? Let's head down memory lane and take a walk together!

-Zoo Tycoon-

From the creators of Roller Coaster Tycoon comes Zoo Tycoon! The business simulator that has you running a zoo! Build exhibits, keep animals happy, make money, make guests happy (or drop them into the lion enclosure!) Anything is possible in Zoo Tycoon!!! With two expansions, an aquarium expansion 'Marine Mania' and dinosaur expansion 'Dinosaur Digs,' the sky is the limit! Zoo Tycoon has a heavy focus on design aesthetics as well as money management and animal education. This game is the only reason I know the difference between different types of terrains and foliage. This game is not limited my fact. If you want, you can have a few mythical creatures as well including Yetis, Bigfoots (Bigfeets?), Mermaids and Unicorns. The art style is charming, the UI is easy to understand and navigate, and the game is entertaining throughout! Unfortunately, I haven't been able to find a port of this game for modern hardware as of yet.

-Secret's of DaVinci: The Forbiden Manuscript-

There's nothing like a little point and click mystery adventure game to keep the mind entertained! You play as Valdo, a young and ambitious man who has been invited to Cloux Manor to look for a rumored manuscript written and then hidden by it's former owner, Leonardo DaVinci. This beautifully rendered puzzle game stands out to me most because of it's amazing design. The 3d rendered environments are richly textured, historically accurate, and beautifully lit. The puzzles are challenging, although not impossible so, and the story is engaging and keeps the player hooked. Secret passage ways, old flying machines, counterfeit paintings, characters with secrets, and a beautiful manor to explore await in Secrets of DaVinci; the Forbidden Manuscript. Unfortunately, the only version of this game I have found is unplayable due to glitches and bugs. We need a port of this game to modern hardware ASAP!

-Marble Blast Gold!-

The second that funky electronic theme song starts to play, you know you're in for a treat. You play as, you guessed it, a marble, trying to complete various timed courses to get to the next level. Using WASD to roll, space to hop, and mouse click to use powerups, you can cruise right along through the trials. Or can you? Some of these levels get pretty challenging with the inclusion of hazards like landmines, tornados, slippery mud, super thin tracks, and huge fans, all looking to throw you off your game and ruin your run. This game is awesome for all ages and has been ported to new harware! Will you dare to complete in this marble death trap? Of course you will!

-Bugdom-

Oh my goodness is this game cute. You play as Rollie McFly, a roly poly bug wearing chunky sneakers, as he goes on a quest to rescue imprisoned ladybugs, wearing pink high heels naturally, from the evil fire ant empire! If there's anything to be said about Bugdom it's that it's incredibly creative in it's level design. From a pond where you hop from lily pad to lily pad trying to not get your blood sucked by little mosquitos, also wearing sneakers by the way, to a dense, claustrophobic honey hive filled with angry bees, to a backyard where you have to avoid getting stomped on by oblivious humans and ride dragonflies like fighter planes, this game undoubtedly gets the most out of it's bug theme. The music is also incredible, with each level having its own theme that perfectly represents its vibe. The controls are a bit stilted, but that's to be expected. This was the first computer game I ever beat. Yep! All 12, honestly pretty difficult after level 3, levels. This ones pretty freakin awesome! Check it out if you can, I believe there are fan ports of it.
