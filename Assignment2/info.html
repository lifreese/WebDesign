Sites to Find Old Games!

  • Itch.io is worth a look to see if someone has made an old game playable! For example, here's an updated version of Bugdom!
  • My Abandonware is a great resource to find old games to play. However, it should be noted that these will take a little more installation set up on your end as opposed to itch.io's runnable files. For example, here's a playable version of Zoo Tycoon!
  • The Video Game History Foundation is a digital library of gaming media, think magazines etc. While I haven't been able to find any playable games, you can still read up on them or look at their source code!

Helpful Content Creators

  • RagnarRox is a content creator who focuses primarily on mostly unknown horror games. He frequently plays writes his video essays about games that are hard to get your hands on. I'd recommend his videos on Rule of Rose and Kuon to get you started!
    • One Short Eye is a creator who focuses on games from the 80s and 90s. Part walkthrough, part behind the scenes fun, what I enjoy about his content is that he frequently goes into the source code of the games to point out how the mechanics work for the viewer. I'd recommend his video on The Dagger of Amon Ra.
  • Lastly, I'd recommend Power Pak. Although he doesn't focus on older games like the other recomendations, Power Pak releases content about a lot of interesting indie projects releasing. I'd recommend his video on Proximate, which we talked about on the About page. I'd also recommend his video on Vile, for a look at an impressive game and some information about Steam sensorship.
